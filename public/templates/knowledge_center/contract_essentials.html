Contract Essentials

Work Time

A contract contains all the points of agreement mutually agreed by parties. Being legally binding, it protects the interests of all sides in case of dispute, and is a proof of their commitment to the collaboration.

Key Points

01

A good contract is one which covers all possible scope of working together. Make it a point to discuss all key points before taking up the work, and formalize them in a written and signed contract.

02

To start with, explicitly mention the work timeline(s), approval process and the budget. Discuss with the client, and put your points and concerns forward wherever required.

03

Quite often, the client sends a work back for making iterations, which can vary from a couple to almost redoing the work itself. In such a case, it is advisable to pre-discuss and include in the contract cost of iterations (if any), along with any after sales support required post the approval.

04

Intellectual Property Rights, licenses and credits on the work are extremely important factors for a creative professional. In hired work, these automatically belong to the commissioner. If you plan on having it otherwise, ensure that it is written in the contract.

05

To avoid a bad experience due to termination, your contract should contain explicit details on the possible manners and effects of termination for both the parties.
