Prevention And Cure

Your creative work is your intellectual property, and understandably, holds significant value. While you are the natural owner of your original work, having a copyright is an advantage in legal enforcement and ensures your work remains yours.

Key Points

01

The creator of a work is its automatic owner as well, implying that the intellectual property rights (IPR) are held by the creator. Absence of a written copyright does not make the work liable for copying. The moment an original work comes to form, copyright is automatically created in the creator/commissioner.

02

It is not mandatory to register your work to have an ownership on it. However, a copyright registration is useful evidence in instances of infringement.

03

Under Indian laws, copyright exists in original literary, dramatic, musical and artistic works, this includes computer programmes, which are treated as “literary work”.

04

When working on a commissioned project, the client/ commissioner becomes the IPR holder. If an artist plans to keep the IPR, then it has to be explicitly mentioned in an agreement.

05

Working on a collaborative work, IPR-related interests can become a source of conflict. In absence of an agreement, the rights and interests arising from the work get distributed equally among the collaborators, meaning that royalties and other monetary benefits from the work are divided in the same proportion as well.

06

The process for registering a copyright in India has been made fairly simple with the e-filing facility. Log on to www.copyright.gov.in, fill up Form IV, and make the necessary payment online. The application charge is around Rs. 5,000 per work.

07

After submitting the application, you are given a diary number post which there is a wait period of 30 days. This buffer period is used to hear any objection or discrepancy regarding your application. After this period is exhausted, it takes about 2 to 3 months for the final registration.

08

If any part of the procedure sounds complicated, you can easily get your copyright filed through a certified agent online or through an advocate.
